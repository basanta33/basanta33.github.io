My name is Basanta Khakurel. 

I am from Kathmandu, Nepal. I moved to Hammond, Louisiana on Fall 2017 for my Bachelor's degree in Biological Sciences from Southeastern Louisiana University. I worked with [Dr. April Wright](https://wright-lab.com/) on eastern Pine snakes for my senior Honor Thesis. By that time I had already started enjoying working in the Wright Lab so I decided to stay there for my Master's. 

For my Master's thesis, I am working on using morphological data to infer phylogenetic tree using Bayesian methods. I am particularly testing a model of evolution for morphological data using Posterior Predictive Simulation and using reversible jump MCMC to choose between models.

After completing my Master's at Southeastern Louisiana University, I will be joining Dr. Sebastian Hoehna's lab in LMU-Munich, Germany.
